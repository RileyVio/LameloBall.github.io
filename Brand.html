Highschool

LaMelo Ball was a highly prolific scorer during his high school career, famously scoring 92 points in a single game for Chino Hills High School as a sophomore.

He averaged roughly 26.7 points and 10 assists as a sophomore and 16.4 points per game as a freshman, winning a state title, before leaving to play professionally.

International

LaMelo Ball’s overseas career featured a brief but difficult stint in Lithuania with Prienu Skycop, averaging 6.5 points in 8 games, followed by a standout 2019-20 NBL season with Australia’s Illawarra Hawks.

In Australia, he averaged 17.0 points, 7.6 rebounds, and 6.8 assists, winning Rookie of the Year before becoming the 3rd pick in the 2020 NBA Draft.

NBA

LaMelo Ball entered the NBA after being selected 3rd overall in the 2020 NBA Draft by the Charlotte Hornets. He made an immediate impact with his flashy passing, confidence, and scoring ability, winning the NBA Rookie of the Year award in 2021. Known for his deep shooting range and creative playmaking, LaMelo quickly became the face of the Hornets franchise. He earned his first NBA All-Star selection in 2022 and continues to develop as one of the league’s most exciting young guards.
